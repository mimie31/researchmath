📖 The Food Security Context

In urban aquaponics systems—a key technology for local food self-sufficiency (*Self-Sufficiency Level - SSL*) in Malaysia—Red Tilapia (*Oreochromis niloticus*) growth must be precisely monitored. Biomass accumulation follows a sigmoidal logistic pathway constrained by carrying capacity.

The First Derivative \( f'(x) \) represents the instantaneous daily growth rate (grams/day). In food security logistics, identifying the day where \( f'(x) \) peaks tells us exactly when the system reaches its maximum production velocity. Harvesting or thinning out fish stocks at the right derivative transition prevents metabolic waste, maximizes water-use efficiency, and stabilizes local food supply chains.

📊 Calculus-Based Yield Model

Total Fish Weight \( f(x) \):
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + \beta \cdot e^{-r \cdot \alpha \cdot x}}$$
Derivative \( f'(x) \) (Biomass Velocity):
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot \beta \cdot (r \cdot \alpha) \cdot e^{-r \cdot \alpha \cdot x}}{(1 + \beta \cdot e^{-r \cdot \alpha \cdot x})^2}$$
\( x \): Grow-out cycle timeline (Days 0 to 120)
\( L \): Carrying Capacity Asymptote (g)
\( \beta \): Fingerling scale constant (fixed 90)
\( r \): Specific intrinsic growth constant
\( \alpha \): Water Quality & Dissolved Oxygen Index (0.6 - 1.2)
